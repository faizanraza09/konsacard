About

Why konsacard.pk exists

Most people in Pakistan pick their dining card by accident — they take whatever their bank offers when they open an account, or they go with what a friend recommends. This site exists to make that decision deliberate instead.

The problem this solves

Pakistan has a surprisingly large number of restaurant discount programs — HBL, Meezan, Bank Alfalah, UBL, and others each run their own offers with their own terms, coverage lists, day restrictions, and discount caps. None of them publish a comparison. Banks have no incentive to tell you a competitor's card is better for the restaurants you actually go to.

The result is that most people either overpay for a premium card they don't need, or underuse a no-fee card that would have been perfectly fine for their habits. A 30-second search rarely surfaces a side-by-side comparison that accounts for your city, your restaurants, and your typical bill size all at once.

What this site actually does

It pulls offer data from public bank pages and partner merchant listings, normalizes it into a consistent format, and lets you run a comparison against your own inputs — the restaurants you care about, the days you go out, and the bill size you're typically splitting.

The ranking is calculated fresh from your inputs each time. A card that wins for one person's habits can rank lower for someone else's. That's intentional — there is no single best card for Pakistan, only the best card for how you specifically dine.

What this site is not

It is not a bank, a card application service, or a financial advisor. It does not earn referral fees or commissions if you apply for a card you find here. Rankings are not sponsored — no issuer pays for placement.

It also isn't a real-time feed. Offers get updated when public sources change, but the final terms for any offer should always be confirmed directly with the bank or merchant before you make a decision based on them.

Who built this

konsacard.pk was built by someone who got tired of manually cross-referencing bank offer pages before deciding where to eat in Karachi. The data pipeline and matching tool started as a personal spreadsheet and turned into this after it became clear others had the same problem.

Start comparing

The tool takes about 30 seconds to run if you already know which restaurants you want to check. Select your city, add a few places, set your bill size, and the ranking handles the rest.
