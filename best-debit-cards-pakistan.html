Debit cards for restaurant discounts in Pakistan

Most Pakistani banks offer restaurant discounts on debit cards, not just credit cards. The right debit card depends on where you eat, how often, and at what bill size. Here's how to find it.

Do Pakistani debit cards actually get restaurant discounts?

Yes — and the programs are more extensive than most people realise. All 18 banks in the konsacard.pk dataset run restaurant discount programs on debit products. The misconception that discounts are credit-card-only comes from how banks market them: credit cards get the flashy banner ads; debit discount pages are often buried in the site.

In practice, discount percentages on debit and credit are often identical at the same tier. A Meezan Platinum Debit and a Meezan Platinum Credit frequently carry the same restaurant deals. The key variable is tier, not card type.

Key insight: If you already bank with HBL, Meezan, Bank Alfalah, MCB, or UBL, you very likely have a debit card that qualifies for meaningful restaurant discounts right now — without applying for anything new.

For pure restaurant savings, debit and credit cards at the same tier are usually equivalent. Debit wins on simplicity and cost; credit wins if you also want purchase rewards or are building a credit profile.

What to look for in a debit card for dining

1
Restaurant coverage, not partner count A card claiming 500 partner restaurants is meaningless if your 5 regulars aren't among them. What matters is whether the card covers the specific places you already go.
2
The cap at your bill size If you typically spend Rs 6,000 per outing, a Rs 500 cap gives you just 8.3% effective discount. You need a card with a cap that doesn't hit until well above your typical spend.
3
Day validity Many dining offers exclude Fridays, Saturdays, or weekends. If you mostly dine out on weekends, eliminate cards with weekday-only terms before comparing percentages.
4
Annual fee vs. savings gap Many Platinum debit cards have annual fees of Rs 3,000–8,000. Ensure the extra savings over your current card covers the fee — otherwise upgrade is a net loss.

Strong debit programs by bank

The following banks have notable debit card restaurant programs. This is not a ranked list — the right card depends on where you eat and which city you're in. Use the tool to get the ranking specific to your restaurants and bill size.

HBL

Broad restaurant network in all three cities. Konnect tier is strong for food delivery apps. Higher tiers (Platinum, World Elite) carry more generous caps at premium restaurants.

Meezan Bank

Strong in Karachi with good coverage. Platinum and World debit tiers offer meaningful caps. World tier has high eligibility requirements but strong deals.

Bank Alfalah

Large partner network. Gold and Platinum debit cards are widely available. Visa Signature debit has premium caps. Strong in both Karachi and Lahore.

MCB Bank

Platinum debit card shows materially higher caps at upscale restaurants vs. Gold. Good coverage in all three cities. Solid mid-tier option.

UBL

Tiered dining discounts published openly: 30% for Signature/Platinum, 20% for Premium/Gold, 15% for standard. Transparent structure makes it easy to compare tiers.

Faysal Bank

Growing restaurant coverage. Strong in Karachi. Worth checking if your restaurants are among their partners.

This list changes. Restaurant partnerships are updated by banks regularly. Run the tool with your specific restaurants to see who currently covers them best — a bank that wasn't competitive last quarter may have added your regular spots.

Common mistakes when choosing a debit card for dining

If you use 5 specific restaurants, a card from a "smaller" bank that covers all 5 of them beats a card from a major bank that covers none of them. Always run the restaurant-specific comparison before deciding.
A 30% card with a Rs 400 cap saves you Rs 400 on a Rs 5,000 bill. A 15% card with a Rs 1,500 cap saves you Rs 750. The lower percentage wins by Rs 350. Always calculate the actual rupee saving at your bill size.
It's common for offers to be valid Monday–Thursday only. If you mainly dine Friday–Sunday, this eliminates a large chunk of what's advertised. Filter by your dining days before comparing cards.
Most bank restaurant discount programs apply to dine-in and takeaway only. Food delivery apps have separate, independently negotiated partnerships that change frequently. Don't assume your dining card discount works on Foodpanda or Careem NOW.
Banks often automatically upgrade customers who meet the balance or salary threshold. Before applying for a new card elsewhere, check if your existing bank has already made you eligible for the next tier up — the upgrade may cost nothing.
