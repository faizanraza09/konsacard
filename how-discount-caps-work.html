How discount caps affect the ranking

Caps are the main reason a lower headline discount can still beat a higher one for your actual bill size.

The basic idea

A 40% discount sounds stronger than 20%, but if the 40% offer is capped at a low amount, it may stop saving meaningfully once your bill crosses a threshold.

Why the bill slider matters

  • At lower bill sizes, headline percentages often matter more.
  • At higher bill sizes, caps can flatten savings quickly.
  • That is why the ranking changes when you move the slider.

What to look for

  1. High discount and high cap is rare and valuable.
  2. Moderate discount with a generous cap can be more reliable.
  3. If a source does not publish a cap, treat the ranking as less certain.
