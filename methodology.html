Methodology

How konsacard.pk ranks cards

This site is built to answer a practical question: which card is most useful for the restaurants, cities, days, and bill sizes you actually care about.

Source approach

Offers are compiled from public bank offer surfaces, official partner pages, and public merchant-discovery pages where card offers are intentionally exposed. Where multiple public sources describe the same offer, the data is normalized into one comparable format.

  • City names are standardized to Karachi, Lahore, and Islamabad.
  • Card labels are normalized so offer rows from different sources can be compared.
  • Caps are included when the source publishes them and left blank when it does not.

Ranking logic

The core ranking is based on estimated PKR savings for your bill, how many of your selected restaurants the card covers, and whether the card matches the days you actually go out.

Estimated savings

The tool estimates how much each card would save on your selected bill size.

Coverage

Cards with broader useful restaurant coverage rank better than narrow one-off winners.

Day fit

If your preferred days are not covered by a deal, that card is penalized.

No sponsorship

No bank pays to appear at the top of a ranking on this site.

Limits of the dataset

  • Some campaigns publish exact caps and exclusions, while others only publish the headline discount percentage.
  • Offers can change without notice, so the final decision should always be confirmed with the issuer or merchant.
  • Rankings are only as current as the latest public update cycle.

Practical reading

The best card for broad dining coverage is not always the same as the best card for one expensive restaurant habit. Use the tool with your own shortlist rather than relying only on city-wide averages.
