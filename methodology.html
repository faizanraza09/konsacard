Methodology

How konsacard.pk ranks cards

Rankings on this site are calculated from your inputs — not editorial picks, not sponsored placements, and not city-wide averages that flatten the differences between how individual people actually use their cards.

Where the data comes from

Offers are compiled from public bank offer pages, official partner merchant listings, and publicly accessible card benefit summaries published by issuers and their dining program partners. No data is scraped behind login walls or taken from private account pages.

When multiple public sources describe the same offer with conflicting details, the most specific source takes precedence — a specific discount cap listed on a bank's official offer page overrides a vague description on an aggregator. Where sources genuinely conflict without a clear hierarchy, the more conservative number is used and the uncertainty is noted.

  • City names are standardized to Karachi, Lahore, and Islamabad. Sub-city references like "DHA" or "Gulberg" are mapped to the parent city unless an offer is explicitly restricted to a neighborhood.
  • Card labels are normalized so the same card referenced under slightly different names across sources maps to a single comparable entry.
  • Discount caps are included when published. When they aren't, the cap field is left blank rather than assumed to be unlimited.
  • Day restrictions are included at the offer level. A card with different terms on weekdays versus weekends is represented as separate offer rows so the ranking reflects the day filter correctly.

How the ranking is calculated

Every ranking is calculated fresh from your specific inputs. There is no fixed ordering that gets applied across all users — the same card can rank first for one person's inputs and fifth for another's.

Estimated PKR savings

For each card, the tool calculates expected savings on your bill size across your selected restaurants. Discount percentages are applied to the bill and then capped at the published cap amount. Where no cap is published, the calculation notes the uncertainty.

Restaurant coverage

Cards are scored on how many of your selected restaurants they cover, not on total city-wide restaurant count. A card that covers all 5 of your selected places scores higher than one that covers 100 city restaurants but misses 4 of your 5.

Day alignment

If you specify preferred dining days, cards are penalized for offers that don't apply on those days. A card that looks strong but is invalid on Friday and Saturday is scored accordingly if you've indicated those are your primary dining days.

No sponsorship

No bank or card issuer pays for placement in the ranking. A card ranks where the calculation puts it based on your inputs — no exceptions and no adjustments for commercial relationships.

What the ranking does not account for

The ranking is designed to answer one narrow question well: which card saves the most money at the specific restaurants you use, on the days you use them, at your bill size. It does not attempt to be a comprehensive card review.

Things the ranking deliberately excludes:

  • Card approval likelihood or income requirements — these are too individual to generalize.
  • Non-dining card benefits like cashback on retail, travel rewards, or lounge access — that's a different comparison.
  • Card quality, customer service, or app experience — these matter but aren't measurable from public offer data.
  • Promotional introductory rates that differ from the published standing offer terms.

How current is the data

The dataset is updated manually when public offer pages change. Bank offer pages in Pakistan update irregularly — sometimes monthly, sometimes quarterly, sometimes with no advance notice. The data reflects the most recent public update cycle, but offers can change at any time.

Before making a card decision based on anything you see here, confirm current terms directly with the bank or via the merchant's posted offer. This site is a comparison tool and a starting point — not a definitive source of record for offer terms.

Use the tool with real inputs

The methodology is designed to reward specificity. A ranking built on your actual restaurant list and real bill size is meaningfully more accurate than one built on city-wide averages. It takes about 30 seconds to set up properly.
